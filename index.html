Experience with Computer Science

My first experience with a computer was my very own gameboy advance back in 2005. I was attending a kindergarten in Korea when my grandparents came back from the trip to the US with a tiny gaming device for me. I played Pokemon for the first time and was fascinated/shocked. Ever since then I would try to familiarize myself with technology. In first grade, I learned that by using my parent's computer and printer, I could print and sell my own rare YuGiOh cards. Looking back, the fake YuGiOh cards I made with a printer was pretty low quality and easy to tell from the real ones, but some of my friends traded me genuine rare cards for my fake ones. This is how I realized the power of technology.

Since then, I have used all different kinds of computers. From Windows XP,7, and 8 to MacBook Pros, I use computers on a daily basis to not only do homework and watch lecture videos, but also to play games and video chat with my friends. I wish I did program on a daily basis, but I cannot honestly say that I do. I have yet to have found the enjoyment in programming/web development and I hope to find it through this course! You might be wondering why I even am a Computer Science major then in the first place. I came to college as a pre med student and did not switch to CS until my Junior year!! What motivated me to switch is the part I enjoy most so far about computer science: the coding problems. Although I haven't had enough experience in programming to enjoy software development or website development, I found solving coding problems and looking for ways to optimize runtime very fun and exciting.

I honestly have no idea what I am going to be doing in terms of programming in the future. One of the things I am very interested in right now is cybersecurity. Maybe I can see myself learning about how to better security for a software product after I have learnt how to build software. So far I have learned Python, Java, Javascript, Swift, and SQL. With this course I hope to gain further knowledge in the languages/skills necessary to build web applications such as HTML, CSS, and Javascript. I hope to be able to become a developer that can create creative and dependable software!
